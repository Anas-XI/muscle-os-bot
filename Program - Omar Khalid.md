## 2. Safety & Risk Assessment

### Safety Triage Flag: YELLOW

**Red-adjacent concern — current binge eating (B2 equivalent):**

According to the vault's Safety Triage (Section B), binge eating episodes occurring regularly (≥1x/week with loss of control) require a Yellow-to-Red classification. This program incorporates the **Gentle Entry Protocol**:
- No calorie counting or macro tracking for the first 4 weeks
- No restriction-focused nutrition (no deficits, no food elimination)
- No food weighing or measuring
- Focus on **food ADDITION** (protein + fiber) rather than subtraction

### Medical History Considerations

**Osgood Schlatter (past):** Traction apophysitis of the tibial tubercle. Typically resolves after growth plate closure (ages 14-18). At 19, unlikely to be active but:
- Monitor for any pain at the tibial tubercle during knee-dominant exercises
- Avoid deep explosive knee extension under heavy load
- Quad-dominant work is safe with controlled ROM

**Osteomalacia (past):** Indicates prior severe vitamin D deficiency with bone softening. Critical implications:
- **Current vitamin D status must be assessed** — past osteomalacia suggests very low levels historically. Recommend bloodwork (serum 25(OH)D).
- Supplement Vitamin D3 2,500-5,000 IU/day + K2 100 mcg/day (no brainer given history)
- Ensure adequate calcium intake (dairy or fortified foods)
- Weight-bearing exercise (which this program provides) is osteogenic

## 5. Nutrition Protocol — Gentle Entry (No Tracking)

### Phase 1: Weeks 1-4 — The Addition Protocol

**Rule: You never subtract. You only ADD.**

| What to ADD | Target | Why |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| **Protein** | Add 1 high-protein food to each of 3 meals | Protein increases satiety, reduces cravings, supports muscle |
| **Fiber** | Add 1 fruit OR vegetable to each meal | Fiber fills stomach, slows glucose, improves gut health |
| **Water** | 1 glass before each meal | Reduces meal volume intake, hydration |
| **Protein shake** | 1 scoop after training | Convenient protein boost without cooking |

**No tracking, no weighing, no logging. Just add.**

### Phase 1: Food Addition Examples

| Meal | Current (likely) | ADD these | Result |
|------|------------------|-----------|--------|
| Breakfast | Cereal or skipped | Add 2 eggs + 1 piece fruit | +15g protein + fiber |
| Lunch | Sandwich or fast food | Add 200g Greek yogurt or chicken breast | +30g protein |
| Dinner | Varies | Add meat/fish portion + 1 cup vegetables | +25g protein + fiber |
| Post-training | Nothing or random | Add protein shake (30g) | +30g protein |

→ **Total protein addition: ~100g/day without tracking**

### Phase 1: Cravings & Binge Management

From Pillar 5 (Behavioral Systems Layer):

1. **The 10-Minute Rule:** When a craving hits, set a timer for 10 minutes. If you still want it after 10 min, have it — but mindfully, sitting down, no distractions.
2. **Pre-emptive eating:** Eat a protein + fiber meal BEFORE situations that trigger binges (studying, stress, boredom).
3. **No banned foods:** Nothing is "off limits". Restriction triggers binge cycles. Include sweets in small, planned amounts.
4. **Sleep + binge link:** Poor sleep quality increases ghrelin (hunger hormone) by up to 28% (Taheri 2004). Fixing sleep quality is binge management.

### Phase 2: Weeks 5-12 — Light Awareness

**Still no strict tracking. Instead:**

1. **Protein awareness:** Aim for ~120-140g/day (1.6-2.0 g/kg). Use hand portions: 2 palm-sized portions of protein per meal.
2. **Hunger scale:** Eat when hungry (3-4/10), stop when comfortably full (6-7/10). Never starve, never stuffed.
3. **Sweet craving protocol:** Allow 1 planned sweet treat per day (200-300 kcal) — after a protein meal, never on empty stomach.

### Phase 3: Weeks 13-16 — Assess

If binge episodes have stopped or reduced significantly:
- Consider light tracking: just protein for 1-2 weeks (no calories)
- Assess whether a small surplus (+150-250 kcal) would accelerate results

If binge episodes continue:
- Do NOT introduce tracking
- Continue Addition Protocol
- Consider professional referral (dietitian specializing in disordered eating)

### Phase 1: Weeks 1-4 — Technique Acquisition

**Rules:**
- All exercises: 2 working sets, RIR 4 (leave 4 reps in reserve)
- Load: Pick a weight where rep 6 of 15 feels challenging but clean
- Rep range: 12-15 (higher reps for technique practice)
- Focus: Mind-muscle connection, controlled eccentric (3s), no momentum
- Record 1 set of each exercise on your phone after Week 2 to check form
- NO training to failure

### Phase 2: Weeks 5-12 — Progressive Overload

**Progression Model:**

| Weeks | Sets | RIR | Rep Range | Notes |
|-------|------|-----|-----------|-------|
| 5-6 | 3 | 3 | 10-15 | Add 1 set per exercise. RIR back to 3. |
| 7-8 | 3 | 2 | 8-12 | Drop rep range. Push closer to stimulus. |
| 9-10 | 3 | 2 | 8-12 | Add load when hitting top of rep range at RIR 2 for 2 sessions |
| 11-12 | 3 | 1-2 | 8-12 | Continued progression |

**Load Progression Rule (from Pillar 2):**
> When the top of the rep range is hit with clean technique at the target RIR for 2 consecutive sessions → add load (2.5 kg on isolation, 5 kg on machine compounds).

**Deload (Week 13 or earlier if):**
- Joint pain (knee) increasing
- Motivation dropping significantly
- Sleep quality declining
- Binge episodes increasing (stress signal)

## 7. Sleep & Recovery Protocol

### Current State: 9h but poor quality

| Dimension | Current | Problem | Fix |
|-----------|---------|---------|-----|
| Duration | 9h ✓ | — | Maintain |
| Timing | Irregular | Disrupts circadian rhythm | Consistent bedtime ±30 min |
| Caffeine | All day | Still active at bedtime when half-life is 5-6h | Cutoff by 2 PM |
| Screens | Bedtime disruptor | Blue light suppresses melatonin | No screens 45 min before bed |

### Caffeine Protocol

Your "all day" caffeine is the single biggest factor destroying sleep quality despite 9h in bed. Even if you fall asleep, caffeine reduces deep sleep (NREM stage 3) by up to 20%.

**Action:**
- Week 1: Move last caffeine to 4 PM
- Week 2: Move to 2 PM
- Week 3+: Keep cutoff at 1-2 PM

If you need a warm drink after 2 PM: herbal tea (peppermint, chamomile, rooibos)

### Light & Screens

- Install blue-light filter on phone (set to activate at 8 PM automatically)
- Last 45 min before bed: no phone/laptop. Alternatives: read a physical book, stretch, journal
- Bedroom: completely dark (cover LEDs, use blackout curtains)

### Morning Routine (Circadian Anchor)

- Wake time: consistent ±30 min (even weekends)
- Morning light: 5-10 min outdoor within 60 min of waking
- No phone in bed (keeps sleep association strong)

## 8. NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis)

Your <5,000 steps/day is the second biggest limiter for recomp. Recomp requires energy balance — if you're mostly sedentary, your maintenance is low, and you can't eat enough to support muscle growth without gaining fat.

### Step Progression

| Weeks | Target Steps | Method |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| 1-2 | 5,000-6,000 | 10-min walk after each meal (3 × 10 min = +2,000 steps) |
| 3-4 | 6,000-7,000 | Add 15-min walk post-training (cool-down walk) |
| 5-8 | 7,000-8,000 | One dedicated 20-min walk on rest days |
| 9-12 | 8,000-10,000 | Walking is now a habit — naturally increase |

**Walking does not:**
- Impair recovery (unlike running or cardio)
- Increase hunger significantly (unlike high-intensity cardio)
- Interfere with leg training (20-min walk is active recovery)

## 10. Binge Eating — Behavioral Protocol

This is the **critical path** of the entire program. Without addressing this, no training or nutrition plan will work.

### From the Vault's Behavioral Systems Layer:

#### Week 1-2: Awareness (No Change)

Just **notice** without judgment:
- Keep a simple log: time, trigger, what you ate, how you felt before/after
- Don't try to change anything yet
- Identify patterns: stress? Boredom? Restriction? Social?

#### Week 3-4: Structure

- **Eat every 3-4 hours.** Long gaps trigger binges.
- **Plate method:** Fill half your plate with veggies/fruit, quarter with protein, quarter with carbs
- **Pre-emptive eating:** Always eat a protein-rich meal before situations that trigger binges

#### Weeks 5-12: The 3-Rule System

1. **No eating from the package.** Portion onto a plate/bowl first.
2. **Sit down, no distractions.** No eating while studying, watching, or scrolling.
3. **The 10-minute pause.** Want a second portion? Wait 10 minutes. Most cravings pass.

#### When a Binge Happens (Not "If" — "When")

Binges are not failures. They are data.

- **Same day:** Don't restrict afterwards. Eat normally at next meal. Restriction guarantees another binge.
- **Day after:** Continue the Addition Protocol. No punishment (no extra cardio, no skipping meals).
- **Pattern analysis:** What was the trigger? (Lack of sleep? Long gap between meals? Stress? Restriction earlier that day?)
