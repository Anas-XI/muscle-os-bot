### Exercise Selection Rationale

Every exercise is chosen based on the **Injury-Training Compatibility Matrix** and **SFR ratings** from the Master Exercise Index.

#### RED (avoid completely):
- Barbell back squat (knee PF compression)
- Barbell overhead press (shoulder impingement)
- Behind-neck pull-down/press
- Upright rows
- Dips (deep ROM — shoulder stress)
- Bulgarian split squats (knee shear)
- Full ROM leg extension (patellofemoral compression)
- Pull-ups (if shoulder painful — substitute lat pulldown)

#### YELLOW (modify technique):
- Leg press — keep ROM where knee is comfortable, high foot position
- DB bench press — use neutral grip, stop at shoulder height
- Rows — use neutral grip, avoid extreme stretch at bottom
- Lunges — short stride only, controlled descent

#### GREEN (safe to progress):
- Hack squat, goblet squat (partial)
- Landmine press, machine shoulder press
- Cable work (fly, lateral raise, face pull, pushdown)
- Hip thrust, RDL, leg curl
- Chest-supported rows

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4) — Acclimation + Rehab

**Rules:**
- All exercises: 2 working sets, RIR 3, RPE 6-7
- Add 1-2 sets of prehab work per session (banded shoulder rotations, knee-on-band walks)
- NO training to failure. NO max attempts.
- Focus: movement quality, mind-muscle connection, joint comfort mapping

### Phase 2: Progressive Bulk (Weeks 5-16)

**Acclimation Exit Criteria (must pass ALL before starting Phase 2):**
- [ ] Gut issues reduced by 50%+ (see Section 4)
- [ ] Sleep at least 7h/night for 2 consecutive weeks
- [ ] Knee pain during training ≤ 2/10
- [ ] Shoulder pain during training ≤ 2/10
- [ ] Weight stable or slightly up on current intake

**Progression Model:**

| Weeks | Sets | RIR | Rep Scheme | Notes |
|-------|------|-----|------------|-------|
| 5-6 | 3 | 2-3 | Same rep ranges | Add 1 set to each exercise |
| 7-8 | 3 | 2 | Same rep ranges | Push closer to stimulus |
| 9-10 | 3-4 | 1-2 | Same rep ranges | Add set to lagging groups |
| 11-12 | 3-4 | 1 | Same rep ranges | Peak volume. Deload after Wk12 |
| 13 (deload) | 2 | 3-4 | 60% load | Full deload week |
| 14-16 | 3 | 2 | Same rep ranges | Second mesocycle |

**Load Progression Rule (from Pillar 2):**
> When the top of the rep range is hit with clean technique at the target RIR for 2 consecutive sessions → add load (2.5-5 kg on compounds, 1-2.5 kg on isolations).

**Deload Trigger (automatic at Week 13, earlier if):**
- Joint pain increasing (knee/shoulder > 4/10)
- Weight gain stalls for 3+ weeks
- Sleep drops below 6h for 5+ days
- Gut symptoms worsen significantly

### Step 1: Find True Maintenance (Weeks 1-2)

Your reported 4000 kcal at 74 kg bodyweight with no weight gain suggests either:
- Tracking inaccuracy — most likely
- Gut malabsorption reducing effective calorie uptake
- Very high NEAT / metabolic rate

**Protocol:**
1. Weigh all food for 2 weeks
2. Track every calorie honestly
3. Weigh in daily (morning, after bathroom, before food/drink)
4. 7-day rolling average weight

If weight is **stable** → that's your effective maintenance (even if absorption is impaired)
If weight is **dropping** → you're below maintenance
If weight is **gaining** → you're above maintenance

### Step 2: Gut Repair Protocol (Weeks 1-4 — CRITICAL)

Gut health is the **gating factor** for the entire bulk. You cannot grow if you can't absorb.

**Elimination (Weeks 1-2):**
Remove these completely:
- Dairy (milk, cheese, whey protein — switch to plant protein or isolate)
- Gluten (wheat, bread, pasta, barley, rye)
- Processed foods (fried, fast food, packaged snacks)
- Artificial sweeteners (sorbitol, sucralose, etc.)
- Excessive vegetable oils (replace with EVOO, avocado, coconut)

**Allowed base diet:**
- Protein: Lean beef, chicken, eggs, white rice protein or pea protein isolate
- Carbs: White rice, potatoes, sweet potatoes, oats (gluten-free), bananas
- Fats: Olive oil, avocado, egg yolks, coconut oil, fatty fish
- Vegetables: Well-cooked leafy greens, carrots, zucchini (avoid raw cruciferous for now)
- Bone broth: 1 cup/day (gut lining repair — collagen, glycine, glutamine)
- Fermented foods: Small amounts of sauerkraut or kimchi (start with 1 tbsp/day)

**Reintroduction (Weeks 3-4):**
- Add ONE food group back every 3 days
- Dairy day 1 → observe 3 days → if no symptoms, keep
- Gluten day 4 → observe 3 days → if no symptoms, keep
- Record symptoms: bloating, gas, stomach pain, energy crash, stool changes

**Supplements for gut health:**
- Magnesium glycinate: 200-400 mg pre-bed (sleep + gut motility)
- Zinc carnosine: 25-50 mg/day (gut lining integrity)
- L-glutamine: 5-10 g/day in 2 divided doses (enterocyte fuel)
- Digestive enzymes with largest meal (if bloating persists)

## 6. Sleep Protocol (Non-Negotiable)

Your 6h with screen disruption is a major recovery limiter at age 20. Testosterone production peaks during sleep.

**Immediate (Cost: $0, ROI: Very High):**
- Consistent wake time: 7:00 AM ± 30 min — 7 days/week (no weekend sleeping in)
- Morning light: 10-15 min outdoor within 60 min of waking
- Caffeine cutoff: 1:00 PM (250 mg = ~3 cups coffee → finish by 1 PM)
- No screens 60 min before bed — OR amber blue-light-blocking glasses

**Week 1-2 (Build the habit):**
- Bedtime: 11:00 PM → wake 7:00 AM = 8h in bed target
- Bedroom: 16-19°C, complete darkness (cover all LEDs)
- Wind-down routine starting at 10:00 PM: dim lights, no phone/laptop

**Week 3+ (Supplement support):**
- Magnesium glycinate 200-400 mg + Glycine 3-5 g — 60 min pre-bed
- Glycine lowers core body temperature and improves sleep onset

**Sleep Debt Repayment:**
- Current: ~6h chronically
- Protocol: 2 weeks of 8h+ per night to repay
- If you can't get 8h, extend time in bed to 8.5h to allow 7.5h+

**Reference:** Leproult 2011 — sleep restriction to 5h reduces testosterone by 10-15%.
